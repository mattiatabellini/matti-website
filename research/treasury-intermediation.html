US Treasury Market Intermediation: Dealer Constraints and Term Premia

Supervisor: [TBD]

Institution: Copenhagen Business School

Status: In progress

Abstract

This MATTIAAAAAAAAAA investigates how dealer balance sheet constraints affect term premia across the US Treasury yield curve. Building on Du, Hébert, and Li (2023), I use local projections (Jordà, 2005) centred on identified stress episodes to estimate maturity-specific impulse response functions. The key intermediation proxies are the GCF–SOFR spread, primary dealer net repo positions (PDFS), and the SIFMA term repo share, regressed against ACM term premia across six maturities.
